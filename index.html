Gaussian RBFNet: Gaussian Radial Basis Functions for Fast and Accurate Neural Fields Representation and Reconstruction

Anonymous Author(s)


We propose Gaussian RBFNet, a novel framework that uses Radial Basis Functions with Gaussian kernels to represent neural fields. We demonstrate its performance in representing 3D geometry, RGB images, and radiance fields.

Source Code Paper

Introduction

In this paper, we present a novel neural representation that is both fast—during training and inference—and lightweight. Our key insight is that the neurons used in traditional MLPs perform only simple computations, consisting of a dot product followed by a ReLU activation. As a result, achieving complex nonlinear function representations typically requires shallow MLPs and high-resolution, high-dimensional feature grids. In this work, we demonstrate that by replacing traditional neurons with Radial Basis Function (RBF) kernels, we can achieve highly accurate representations of diverse signals, including 2D (RGB images), 3D (geometry), and 5D (radiance fields), using just a single layer of such neurons. Our proposed representation is highly parallelizable, leverages low-resolution feature grids, and offers a compact, memory-efficient solution.
